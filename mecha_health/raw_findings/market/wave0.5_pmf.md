# Mecha Health - Wave 0.5: Product-Market Fit (PMF) Audit

## PMF Level Assessment: Level 2 (Developing PMF)
Mecha Health is currently operating at **Level 2 (Developing PMF)**, often referred to as "Lite PMF." They have successfully transitioned out of the pure "Nascent" phase by securing significant, repeatable pilot partnerships with major enterprise players (e.g., AmeriRad and an unnamed "largest privately owned radiology practice"). However, they are still in the early stages of building a fully scalable, self-serve sales motion.

## 1. The 3 Dimensions of PMF

### 1.1 Satisfaction (The "Painkiller" Test)
- **Signal**: Extremely High. 
- **Evidence**: Radiologist burnout is a universally recognized crisis (55% burnout rate, projected 3.2M workforce shortage by 2026). Mecha Health’s solution is not a "nice-to-have" analytics dashboard; it is a direct throughput multiplier. Their early pilot data shows an increase from reading 1 scan per hour to 1 scan every 5 minutes. 
- **Trust Factor**: The company has explicitly addressed the "Black Box" problem by making their foundation models fully interpretable, tracing findings back to the visual source. This is critical for clinical satisfaction.

### 1.2 Demand (Sales Velocity & Inbound)
- **Signal**: Developing/Strong.
- **Evidence**: As a YC Winter 2025 startup, they have already closed deals with top-tier teleradiology companies. The fact that they raised a $4.1M Seed round led by Valia Ventures indicates strong investor validation of their early pipeline. The demand is heavily enterprise-focused (B2B).

### 1.3 Efficiency (Unit Economics)
- **Signal**: Developing.
- **Evidence**: With a hyper-lean team of ~5 people (mostly the founding PhDs), their Burn Multiple is likely very low. Because their proprietary models (Mecha Net XR) are "two orders of magnitude smaller" than generalist models from Google/Microsoft, their inference costs (GPU burn) will be significantly lower than competitors relying on massive LLMs, creating a strong pathway to high Gross Margins.

## 2. The 4 Levers (The 4Ps) Evaluation
To move from Level 2 to Level 3 (Strong PMF / "The Geyser"), Mecha Health is primarily pulling the **Product** and **Persona** levers:
- **Product**: Transitioning from single-modality (X-Ray) to complex, multi-modality workflows (CT Volumes) to increase their total addressable contract value per hospital.
- **Persona**: Targeting Teleradiology firms first (where throughput directly equals revenue) before attempting to penetrate slower-moving legacy hospital IT systems.
