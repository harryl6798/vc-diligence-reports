# Mecha Health - Wave 4: Market Dynamics & Displacement Economics

## The Synthesis Mandate (TAM / SAM / SOM Modeling)
- **Atomic Fact 1**: U.S. Radiology workforce is ~41,000, but experiencing a 50% attrition surge due to burnout.
- **Atomic Fact 2**: Total U.S. imaging volume is ~700 million scans annually (600M X-rays, 100M CT scans).
- **Atomic Fact 3**: Rad AI and Aidoc charge between $6 to $40 per scan for enterprise contracts (or $100k+ flat fees).
- **Synthesis (TAM)**: Assuming an average platform fee of $5 per scan across 700M U.S. scans, the immediate domestic TAM for automated reporting is **$3.5 Billion**.
- **Synthesis (SAM)**: Focusing purely on X-rays (Mecha Net XR's beachhead) at 600M scans, the Serviceable Addressable Market is **$3.0 Billion**.

## Hypothesis-First: Disrupted Market & Incumbents
- **Disrupted Market**: The "Administrative/Reporting" workflow of radiology, specifically the gap between image capture and the final dictated report.
- **The "Slow Giants" (Incumbents)**:
    - **Nuance / PowerScribe (Microsoft)**: The legacy dictation software. Vulnerability: It requires the radiologist to manually speak every finding.
    - **Aidoc**: Series E triage leader. Vulnerability: It flags emergencies (e.g., stroke) but doesn't write the full structural report.
- **The Emerging Challenger**:
    - **Rad AI**: The closest direct competitor (Series C, $153M funded). Vulnerability: Rad AI relies heavily on standard LLMs to summarize dictated "Findings" into an "Impression." It does not currently reason directly from the raw pixel data to generate the *entire* report from scratch like Mecha Net does.

## Displacement Economics & Exit Benchmarks
- **The Mecha Wedge**: Mecha Health displaces Rad AI by eliminating the dictation step entirely. The radiologist moves from "Creator" to "Editor."
- **Exit Comps (The Prize)**: In 2024/2025, the AI imaging space saw massive consolidation. GE HealthCare acquired MIM Software and Icometrix; Tempus acquired Paige ($81M). Direct competitor Rad AI is valued at over $500M. If Mecha Net successfully captures the teleradiology market, a near-term exit benchmark is **$100M - $300M** via acquisition by a legacy PACS provider (e.g., Philips, GE) seeking to integrate an "AI-first" reporting engine natively.
